Objectives

The four main components of the research project



Sex Education Platform

Aims to tackle the challenges of sexual education in Sri Lanka by providing accurate, age-appropriate content through a personalized platform. Using machine learning and image processing, it tailors lessons based on age and marital status, while ensuring user privacy and safety through secure registration and partnerships with educational institutions.

Sexual Harassment Reporting

To enhance the effectiveness and user acceptance of voice recognition in a sexual harassment prevention app, the research focuses on creating a model that accurately recognizes distress signals while minimizing false alarms. This ensures reliable activation during emergencies and reduces unnecessary alerts, improving user trust and app functionality.

Blogs and Questionnaire

The platform combines content moderation, machine learning, and community oversight to ensure inappropriate material is prevented in sex education blogs and questionnaires. Filters, NLP, and community reporting systems are used, with machine learning refining detection, while users are educated on creating responsible content.

Sexual Health Platform

This integrate an image processing module within EduGuard to detect STDs like Syphilis, Gonorrhoea, Chlamydia, and Genital Warts using images of external body parts. This allows users to privately assess their sexual health, receive personalized clinic recommendations, and access educational resources to promote early detection and prevention.

Methodology
